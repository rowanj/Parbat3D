Dr. Arko Lucieer is a lecturer and researcher in Geographical Information Science (GIS) and remote sensing at the Centre for Spatial Information Science (CenSIS), University of Tasmania, Australia.

He finished his PhD research at the International Institute for Geo-Information Science and Earth Observation (ITC) in Enschede, The Netherlands, in October 2004. His research focused on modelling and visualising uncertainty in remote sensing image classification and segmentation. His background is an M.Sc. in physical geography at Utrecht University with a specialization in GIS and remote sensing.

Dr. Lucieer wrote the first Parbat tool (currently at version 0.32) to further his research. He proposed and sponsored the Parbat3D project through the University of Tasmania's School of Computing, with the intent of obtaining a fast and flexible base from continuing his work in image classification.

His website is http://www.lucieer.net/
