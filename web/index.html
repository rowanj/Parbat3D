Overview

Satellite imagery is increasingly being used for mapping, observations of climate change and Earth surface feature detection. Numerous satellites are orbiting the Earth to map and monitor land cover, weather, topography, geology, vegetation, oceans and ice.

The advance in technology has resulted in satellite sensors that can detect objects smaller than one metre and sensors that can record information from different parts of the electromagnetic spectrum (visible, infrared, near infrared, and radar).

These technical advances have resulted in huge amounts of data in the form of large image files consisting of millions of pixels in several bands (far more than your average digital camera!)

Efficient display of these images has become a challenge and visualisation is very important for effective analysis of satellite imagery.
