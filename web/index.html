Overview

Satellite imagery is increasingly being used for Earth observation, mapping, observations of climate change, and feature detection. Numerous satellites are orbiting the Earth to map and monitor land cover, weather, topography, geology, vegetation, oceans, and ice. The advance in technology has resulted in satellite sensors that can detect objects smaller than one meter and sensors that can record information from different parts of the electromagnetic spectrum (visible, infrared, thermal infrared, and radar).

Examples of satellites include: NOAA AVHRR (optical, 1km pixel size), Landsat (optical and thermal, 30m pixel size), SPOT (optical, 15m pixel size), IKONOS (optical, 1m pixel size), QuickBird (optical, 0.6m pixel size), RADARSAT (radar, 25m pixel size), ASAR (radar, 12m pixel size).

These technical advances have resulted in huge amounts of data in the form of large image files consisting of millions of pixels in several bands (far more than your average digital camera!). Efficient display of these images has become a challenge and visualisation is very important for effective analysis of satellite imagery.

Parbat3D is an open-source standalone application in C++. Parbat 3D will be able to:
• Drape large satellite images over a Digital Elevation Model (DEM)
• Fly over the terrain in real-time
• Select regions of interest (ROI) by digitising polygons
• Extract image properties from ROIs, such as basic statistical information, shape and textural characteristics.
• Visualise three bands of the image colour composite in an interactive 3D feature space cube, i.e. 3D scatter plot
• Visualise the statistical properties of selected ROIs as 3D clusters or volumes in the 3D plot to visually explore class clusters.
